Must Eats

Ecuador’s regions are close on a map, but they taste completely different. The Andes brings hearty comfort food built for cool mountain mornings. The Coast is bold, citrusy, and seafood-forward. The Amazon leans into native ingredients and traditional preparation. The Galapagos keeps it simple with island flavors and fresh catch. Try one must-eat in each region and you will feel how geography, climate, and culture shape every bite.

What this page is best for

If you want the fastest way to understand Ecuador, eat by region. You will notice the same building blocks show up across the country, like plantain, potatoes, corn, fresh herbs, and citrus, but each ecosystem transforms them into something totally different. Use this page to pick a region, choose one must-eat, and build the rest of your day around it.

Simple planning flow

1) Pick a region you are visiting. 2) Choose one dish as your anchor. 3) Add a second bite that contrasts it, like soup vs roasted, or citrus vs smoky. 4) Save the final decision for when you see the menu and the catch of the day. In the future, each dish will include where to try it and featured spots you can book or visit.

Top must eats by region

Each region has its own logic. The Andes leans warm and filling. The Coast leans bright and ocean-driven. The Amazon leans smoky, wrapped, and ingredient-forward. The Galapagos leans simple and fresh. Try the same ingredient across regions and you will see how Ecuador’s geography changes everything.
