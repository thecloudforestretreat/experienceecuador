The Andes

Ecuador’s highlands are a blend of historic cities, indigenous markets, dramatic volcano landscapes, and scenic drives. Start here if Quito is your arrival hub or if you want culture, altitude, and classic Ecuadorian views.

What defines the Andes

The Andean region runs north to south through the heart of Ecuador. It’s where colonial architecture meets highland life, and where many travelers get their first “wow” moments: mountain viewpoints, volcano backdrops, and deep cultural traditions.

Signature highlights

  • UNESCO-listed historic centers and colonial streets
  • Indigenous markets, textiles, and living traditions
  • Volcano landscapes and national parks (Cotopaxi region)
  • Day trips from Quito: equator, viewpoints, nearby nature
Culture Cities Volcanoes Highlands

Best for

  • First-time Ecuador travelers arriving via Quito
  • Culture, architecture, and historic walking days
  • Volcano scenery and classic highland drives
  • Short itineraries (1–3 days) or longer loops

Planning tips

  • Altitude affects everyone differently, start slow and hydrate
  • Group stops by area to reduce transit time
  • Plan big viewpoints early for clearer skies
  • Keep one flexible block per day for traffic and rest
